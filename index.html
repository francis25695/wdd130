Atiak, Amuru, Uganda

Atiak is a growing town located in the Acholi sub-region of Northern Uganda, within Amuru District. It lies along the important Gulu-Nimule Road, a major trade route connecting Uganda to South Sudan, making the town strategically important for regional trade and transport. Atiak is approximately 71 kilometers north of Gulu City and about 35 kilometers south of the South Sudan border town of Nimule. Historically, Atiak is widely remembered for the tragic 1995 Atiak Massacre, during which the Lord’s Resistance Army (LRA) killed hundreds of civilians and abducted many children. The incident remains one of the darkest moments in Northern Uganda’s history and symbolizes the suffering experienced during the LRA insurgency. Today, Atiak is steadily transforming into an important center for agriculture, commerce, and development. One of the major investments in the area is the Atiak Sugar Factory Limited, a large sugar-processing project established to promote economic growth and create employment opportunities in Northern Uganda. The factory has supported thousands of out-grower farmers and contributed to improving livelihoods in the region. The town also serves as a cultural and administrative center, hosting Atiak Town Council offices, local markets, and the headquarters of the Atiak Chiefdom. Despite its painful history, Atiak continues to rebuild and develop, symbolizing resilience, recovery, and hope for the people of Northern Uganda.
